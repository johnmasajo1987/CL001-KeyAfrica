Our Story

Key Africa’s venture into the healthcare sector in Africa began with a more general exploratory trip, hoping to find business opportunities for Israeli investors. As we traveled around Tanzania in 2018 and got to know the country better, our eyes were opened wide to the state of the country’s healthcare system.


Tanzania’s healthcare industry landscape is one filled with contradictions. On the one hand, we bore witness to ill-equipped hospitals with unsanitary conditions; on the other, we saw towering medical facilities funded by foreign money that was physically and economically out of reach for the common Tanzanian. We saw upscale medical equipment languishing unused in hospitals due to a lack of knowledgeable personnel to operate them.


But with these gaps and contradictions comes great opportunity, and a way to dramatically improve the country’s healthcare infrastructure and the public’s access to it. Our founders re-calibrated Key Africa’s focus and came up with a plan to open Africa’s doors to world-class healthcare, starting in Tanzania.


We collaborated with Terem, Israel’s most renowned primary healthcare provider, to facilitate skills and technology transfer to local healthcare workers, as we worked closely with individuals and companies looking to invest in the rapidly growing African healthcare market. We see this as a way to increase local workforce knowledge, help the communities gain better healthcare access, and offer investors a once-in-a-lifetime investment opportunity.


The Key Africa footprint is top-quality care with deep community roots; a quality of care that is sensitive to the nuances of the communities it serves. We believe that this is our recipe for long-term success in the continent and a genuinely repeatable and financially viable formula.


Key Africa is in Africa to stay. We view the improvement of African healthcare services more than just a business; we view it as a vocation. It is a commitment to a community we want to help, serve, and improve using our entrepreneurial knowledge, business network, and community connections.
