The Luther Hospital

The Luther Hospital is Key Africa's flagship project. It is a healthcare facility aimed at providing primary healthcare and emergency services to the community it shall serve. The Luther Hospital shall open in September 2021.


Located in the Ubungo District of Dar Es Salaam, the Luther Hospital shall give more than 6 million people access to the Key Africa healthcare experience. With its strategic location, it also gives access to the nearby regions and community.


Operated in partnership with Terem, Israel's leading primary care provider, and the Tanzanian Lutheran Church, the facility shall offer various primary care, accidents and emergencies, and laboratory services at a reasonable cost and top-notch delivery. The Tanzanian Lutheran Church shall run and operate a charity program to give the community's indigents access to world-class healthcare. Terem shall provide the facility's staffing requirements, and shall also facilitate the training of local personnel.


The facility shall be managed by Marcelo Gal, an accomplished healthcare management professional. He is the former hospital administrator of the Centro Medical La Paz in Malabo, Equatorial Guinea, where he served for more than half a decade.


Initially built as a primary care facility, the Luther Hospital shall expand into a larger medical facility that shall offer in-facility hospitalizations and other treatments designed to meet the more complex medical needs of the community.
